WHAT IS NATURAL REFERENCING?

Natural referencing, or SEO (Search Engine Optimization), encompasses all the methods and techniques that aim to position the web pages of your website in the first natural results of search engines (especially Google, but also Bing, Yahoo and others). The aim is to improve the visibility of the pages in question by applying a series of "rules" laid down by the search engines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Definition of SEO

SEO is a 3 letter acronym for Search Engine Optimization. Either in French "Optimisation pour les moteurs de recherche" (Search Engine Optimisation) or in English "Optimisation pour les moteurs de recherche" (Search Engine Optimisation)... we are well on our way! In short, it is the optimisation of a website in order to reach the first places of results on the different search engines (generally, Google, Yahoo and Bing). SEO, commonly known as "referencing" or "natural referencing" brings together all the optimisation techniques that make a site more visible and understandable to search engine robots.

Why is SEO important for my site?

Today Google generates 40,000 searches per second, or 3.5 billion searches daily, and represents nearly 90% of the search volume in France. You have millions of internet users who query search engines through different keywords when they are looking for information or a product. A good SEO is the key to success in order to have a place in the top 3 of a result page. It is therefore essential to be present among the first 3 results proposed on your expressions if you want to acquire qualified traffic and make sales. Hundreds of thousands of websites have made a fortune just by positioning themselves in natural referencing! To do this you must have a site that has been done properly, that is well optimised and that respects the main SEO criteria of Google.

How long does it take for a web page to be referenced on Google (indexed)?

There is officially no time set by Google to start indexing your website. This time can vary from a few minutes to several weeks depending on many criteria such as: - The popularity of the site (some call it linking) - The ability of the site to be crawled by robots - The level of technical optimisation of the code - The structure of internal links - The quality of the content In short, the time it takes to be indexed on Google remains quite variable and enigmatic for many SEO agencies and e-marketing professionals. However, the average duration observed is about 2 days to 1 week. In order to speed up this time, we advise our clients to publish regularly on social networks and to be active enough from the launch of their site to accelerate the indexing by search engines and by deduction their referencing.

How do I know if my site is well optimised for SEO?

If you want to know if your site is well optimised there is a very simple way to find out. Simply switch to "private browsing" mode on your browser to avoid possible personalisation of the results, then type in the various expressions and keywords you have worked hard on on google.fr to be present and see where you appear in the engine results. If the results are not there, it means that the work has been badly done beforehand ...

How has SEO evolved since the beginnings of Google?

SEO is a complex discipline that has evolved a lot since its creation (creation of Google in 1998). The Montain View firm is constantly changing its algorithm (by small touches) and SEO has to evolve with these changes. It is rumoured that more than 600 updates of the algorithm are made every year. So the techniques evolve and succeed one another. Beware, Google is tracking more and more those who seek to take shortcuts and freedoms with its guidelines.
